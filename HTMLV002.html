Manali

Manali is a popular tourist destination in the Himalayan state of Himachal Pradesh, India:

Location

Manali is located in the Kullu Valley, about 170 miles north of Shimla and 338 miles northeast of New Delhi.

Known for

Manali is known for its snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and cool climate. It's also a gateway to the Lahaul and Spiti district and Leh in Ladakh.

Activities

Manali offers many activities, including:

Adventure sports:

White water rafting, rowing, river crossing, paragliding, and skiing .

Trekking:

Manali has many trekking options, from easy to challenging.

Exploring local culture:Visit the charming villages and temples to learn about the local lifestyle and culture

Climate

The best time to visit Manali depends on the season:

September–February:

Winter sets in, and temperatures can drop to -1°C

December–January:

A popular time for honeymooners and skiing

March–June:

Summer begins, and temperatures are usually between 10°C–25°C

July–August:

Monsoons can bring heavy rainfall and landslides

History

Manali is said to be the abode of Manu, the first man and lawgiver, according to Hindu mythology. The town's name is derived from "Manu-Alaya," which means "the abode of Manu".

Culture

Manali is a cultural melting pot, with influences from Aryans, Buddhists, and the local population.
