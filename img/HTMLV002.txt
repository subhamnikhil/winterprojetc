<H3>Location</H3>
<P>
Manali is located in the Kullu Valley, about 170 miles north of Shimla and 338 miles northeast of New Delhi.

<H3>Known for</H3>
<P>
Manali is known for its snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and cool climate. It's also a gateway to the Lahaul and Spiti district and Leh in Ladakh. 
</P>

<H2>SHIMLA</H2>

<IMG STYLE="WIDTH:65%"SRC=SHIMLA.JPG>

<P>Shimla is the capital of Himachal Pradesh and was once the summer capital of British India. It's a popular tourist destination known for its scenic location, colonial architecture, and natural beauty: </P>

<H3>Location</H3>
<P>Shimla is built on several hills and ridges, including Jakhu, Prospect Hill, Observatory Hill, Elysium Hill, and Summer Hill. It's located at 30° 6' North latitude and 77° 11' East longitude, with a mean elevation of 2,397.59 meters above sea level. </P>

<H3>Climate</H3>
<P>Shimla's average summer temperature is 16–25 °C (61–77 °F), and the average winter temperature is 3–11 °C (37–52 °F). Snowfall has been happening in January or early February in recent years. </P>

<IMG STYLE="WIDTH:65%"SRC="SHIMLA(2).JPG">

<H3>Attractions</H3>
<P>Shimla has many attractions, including:</P>

<H5>The Mall:</H5><P>A shopping and dining area in the center of town .</P>

<H5>Scandal Point:</H5><P> A popular spot for watching the sunset and taking in the views.</P>

<H5>Jakhu Temple:</H5><P>A temple dedicated to the monkey god, Hanuman, with views of the surrounding valleys and snow-capped peaks. </P>

<H5>State Museum:</H5><P> A museum housed in a colonial mansion with a collection of ancient and contemporary art. </P>

<H5>Viceregal Lodge:</H5><P>A grand old building on Observatory Hill. </P>

<H5>Iron lamp posts:</H5><P>Charming lamp posts that are part of Shimla's colonial heritage. </P>

<H3>History</H3>
<P>Shimla was discovered by the British in 1819 and became the summer capital of India in 1864. The town was built to provide a safe place for soldiers to rest after the Gorkha War. </P>
